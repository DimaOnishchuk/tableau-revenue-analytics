An interactive Tableau project that analyzes revenue performance for a SaaS company operating across multiple locations and software products.

The project covers monthly revenue trends, location and product performance, paid-user metrics, New MRR, month-over-month revenue growth, and cohort analysis.

## Live dashboard

**[Open the interactive dashboard on Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/views/tableau-saas-revenue-analytics/Dashboard_2?:language=en-US&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link

## Business objective

The dashboards were created to help a product team answer the following questions:

- How does revenue change over time?
- Which locations generate the most revenue?
- Which software products perform best?
- How many users make payments each month?
- What is the average revenue per paid user?
- How much revenue is generated by newly converted paid users?
- How does revenue change compared with the previous month?
- How does cohort revenue develop after a user's first payment?

## Dashboard previews

### Dashboard  — Revenue Overview

Shows monthly revenue, monthly revenue by location, and total revenue by software product.

![Revenue overview dashboard](images/dashboards/dashboard-01-revenue-overview.png)
The first dashboard provides a high-level overview of the company’s revenue performance. Its main purpose is to help stakeholders quickly understand how total revenue changes over time, how different locations contribute to the overall result, and which software products generate the largest share of income.

The dashboard combines three visualizations:

#### Monthly Revenue

This line chart shows the total amount of revenue generated in each month. It helps identify the overall revenue trend, periods of rapid growth, temporary declines, and the month with the highest result.

The visualization makes it possible to answer questions such as:

Is the company’s revenue growing over time?
Which months showed the strongest performance?
Were there any noticeable drops in revenue?
Is the growth stable or volatile?

#### Monthly Revenue by Location

This multi-line chart displays monthly revenue separately for APAC, EMEA, and USA. Each line represents one location, making it possible to compare regional performance over time.

The chart helps stakeholders understand:

which location generates the most revenue;
which regions are growing faster;
whether a revenue decline affects all locations or only one market;
how stable the revenue contribution of each location is.

#### Total Revenue by Product

This bar chart compares the total revenue generated by each software product. It provides a clear ranking of products and helps identify the company’s strongest and weakest revenue sources.

The visualization shows that Main App and Customer Success generate the highest revenue. Marketing Automation also represents a significant part of the company’s income, while Publishing has the smallest contribution.

This information can help management:

prioritize the most profitable products;
identify products that need additional promotion;
review pricing or positioning;
allocate development and marketing resources more effectively.

#### Business value of Dashboard 

Dashboard  is designed for management-level monitoring. It provides a quick summary of the company’s financial performance without requiring users to analyze detailed transaction-level data.

It helps stakeholders:

monitor overall revenue growth;
compare regional performance;
identify the most profitable products;
detect unusual monthly changes;
decide which locations or products require deeper analysis.

### Dashboard  — Revenue & User Metrics

Compares revenue by location and software, monthly product trends, location totals, ARPPU, and paid-user count.

![Location, product and user dashboard](images/dashboards/dashboard-02-location-product-users.png)

The second dashboard provides a more detailed analysis of the company’s revenue structure. While the first dashboard focuses on the general financial overview, this dashboard explains where revenue comes from and how customer activity affects monetization.

It combines four visualizations that analyze revenue by location, product, month, and paid-user metrics.

#### Revenue by Location & Software

This clustered bar chart compares total revenue for every combination of location and software product.

The chart helps identify:

which products perform best in each location;
which product-location combinations generate the most revenue;
whether the same products are successful in all regions;
which markets may require a different product or sales strategy.

The results show that product performance varies significantly between locations. Marketing Automation is the strongest product in the USA, while Customer Success generates the highest revenue in APAC. In EMEA, the revenue contribution is more evenly distributed between products.

This means that the company should not use the same sales and marketing strategy for every location. Each market has a different product structure and may require separate promotional activities.
Revenue by Month & Software

This multi-line chart shows monthly revenue trends for each software product.

Each line represents a product, allowing users to compare how product performance changes over time. The chart helps determine whether total revenue growth is driven by one particular product or by several products at the same time.

It answers questions such as:

Which product has the strongest monthly growth?
Which product generates the most stable revenue?
Are any products losing revenue over time?
Which products contributed to the monthly revenue peak?

Main App and Customer Success generally generate the highest monthly revenue. Marketing Automation remains relatively stable, while Publishing has the lowest monthly contribution.

This visualization is useful for product managers because it allows them to monitor product performance and identify when a product starts gaining or losing momentum.

#### Revenue by Location

This bar chart compares total revenue across APAC, EMEA, and USA.

USA is the strongest location and generates the largest share of total company revenue. APAC is the second-largest market and demonstrates strong growth potential. EMEA generates the lowest total revenue.

The chart provides a simple location ranking and helps management understand the geographical structure of the company’s income.

It can support decisions related to:

regional sales targets;
marketing budget allocation;
market expansion;
staffing and operational resources;
investigation of underperforming regions.
ARPPU & Paid Users Count

This dual-axis chart compares two important metrics:

#### ARPPU — Average Revenue Per Paid User;
Paid Users Count — the number of unique users who made a payment during the month.

The chart shows that the number of paid users increased significantly during the analyzed period. At the same time, ARPPU gradually decreased.

This means that the company successfully expanded its paying customer base, but the average revenue generated by each paid user became lower.

Possible explanations include:

the acquisition of more customers on lower-priced plans;
discounts or promotional offers;
a larger share of small customers;
lower average transaction values;
a change in the product mix;
rapid user growth that outpaced revenue growth.

This visualization is important because an increase in paid users does not always mean an improvement in monetization quality. The company needs to track both user growth and average revenue per user.

#### Business value of Dashboard 

Dashboard provides a deeper understanding of revenue drivers and customer monetization.

It helps stakeholders:

identify the strongest product-location combinations;
compare product trends over time;
rank locations by revenue;
monitor paid-user growth;
evaluate whether monetization improves or declines;
detect markets and products that need further investigation.

This dashboard can be especially useful for product managers, regional managers, sales teams, and marketing teams.

### Dashboard  — MRR & Cohort Analysis

Combines New MRR, total revenue with month-over-month change, and a cohort revenue heat map.

![MRR, revenue growth and cohort dashboard](images/dashboards/dashboard-03-mrr-growth-cohorts.png)

The third dashboard focuses on the quality and sustainability of revenue growth. It helps stakeholders understand how much revenue comes from newly acquired paying users, how total revenue changes from month to month, and whether users continue generating revenue after their first payment.

The dashboard combines New MRR, month-over-month revenue change, and cohort analysis.

#### New MRR

The New MRR chart shows the amount of revenue generated by users during the same calendar month in which they made their first payment.

This metric helps measure the financial contribution of newly acquired paying users.

The chart answers questions such as:

How much revenue comes from new paying users each month?
Which month had the strongest customer acquisition result?
Is revenue from new users increasing or decreasing?
Are marketing and sales activities generating enough new revenue?

A high New MRR value may indicate successful acquisition campaigns, strong conversion, or effective onboarding. A decline in New MRR may indicate weaker demand, lower conversion rates, or reduced marketing effectiveness.

It is important to note that New MRR measures revenue from new paying users only. It does not include revenue from existing customers who continue making payments.

#### Total Revenue and Revenue Change

This dual-axis chart combines two indicators:

Total Revenue, displayed as bars;
monthly Revenue Change, displayed as a percentage line.

The bars show the absolute revenue amount for each month, while the line shows how much revenue increased or decreased compared with the previous month.

This combination is useful because absolute revenue and revenue growth describe different aspects of performance.

For example, a month may have high total revenue but still show a negative percentage change if the previous month was stronger. Similarly, a smaller month may show a high growth rate if it increased significantly compared with the previous period.

The chart helps users:

identify months with the strongest growth;
detect significant revenue declines;
compare absolute performance with growth speed;
recognize periods of acceleration or slowdown;
investigate unusual monthly changes.

The strongest positive revenue growth occurred in August 2022, while the largest negative change occurred in December 2022. Revenue reached its highest level in March 2023 and then declined during the following months.

#### Cohort Analysis

The cohort table groups users according to the month in which they made their first payment.

The structure of the table is:

rows represent the user’s first payment month;
columns represent the number of months since the first payment;
cell values show the revenue generated by the cohort;
the color gradient compares revenue with the cohort’s first month.

This allows users to track how revenue from each cohort changes over time.

The cohort table helps answer questions such as:

Do new customers continue generating revenue after their first month?
Which customer cohorts have the strongest long-term performance?
Which cohorts lose revenue quickly?
Are newer cohorts performing better than older cohorts?
Is the quality of acquired users improving over time?

Darker cells represent stronger revenue performance, while lighter cells indicate a decline compared with the cohort’s first month.

A cohort that maintains strong revenue over several months may represent customers with better retention, higher engagement, or stronger long-term value. A cohort with a rapid decline may indicate weak retention or one-time purchases.

#### Business value of Dashboard 

Dashboard  helps evaluate not only how much revenue the company generates, but also how sustainable that revenue is.

It helps stakeholders:

measure revenue from newly acquired users;
monitor monthly revenue growth;
detect periods of acceleration and decline;
analyze customer retention through revenue behavior;
compare the long-term value of different cohorts;
evaluate the quality of customer acquisition;
identify whether business growth depends mainly on new users or returning users.

This dashboard is particularly useful for product, marketing, customer success, and management teams because it connects customer acquisition with long-term revenue performance.

#### Dashboard Filters 

All dashboards include interactive filters that allow users to explore the data in more detail.

The available filters include:

Location;
Software Name;
Payment Date.

The location filter allows users to focus on APAC, EMEA, or USA.

The software filter allows users to analyze a specific product, such as Main App, Customer Success, Marketing Automation, or Publishing.

The date filter allows users to limit the analysis to a selected month or time period.

The filters are configured to affect all relevant visualizations within the dashboard. This allows stakeholders to move from a general company-level overview to a detailed analysis of a specific region, product, or period.

For example, a user can select USA and Marketing Automation to analyze the strongest product-location combination, or select APAC to review revenue trends and cohort performance for that region only.

## Dataset

The analysis uses [`data/saas_revenue.csv`](data/saas_revenue.csv).

| Characteristic | Value |
|---|---:|
| Transactions | 123,195 |
| Date range | 2022-06-01 — 2023-05-30 |
| Unique paid users | 8,463 |
| Locations | 3 |
| Software products | 4 |
| Total revenue | 1,332,835 |
| Overall revenue per paid user | 157.49 |

### Source fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `user_id` | Anonymized user identifier |
| `payment_date` | Transaction date |
| `location` | Customer location: APAC, EMEA, or USA |
| `software_name` | Purchased software product |
| `is_enterprise_customer` | Enterprise customer flag |
| `revenue_amount` | Transaction revenue amount |

## Core metrics

| Metric | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Total Revenue** | Sum of `revenue_amount` |
| **Paid Users Count** | Distinct count of `user_id` |
| **ARPPU** | Total Revenue divided by Paid Users Count |
| **New MRR** | Revenue generated in the same calendar month in which a user made their first payment |
| **Revenue Change** | Percentage change in Total Revenue compared with the previous month |
| **Cohort Revenue Change** | Revenue in a cohort month relative to that cohort's first payment month |

The exact Tableau formulas are documented in [`docs/tableau-calculations.md`](docs/tableau-calculations.md).

## Key insights

- Total revenue for the analyzed period is **1,332,835** from **123,195 transactions** and **8,463 unique paid users**.
- **USA** is the highest-revenue location with **643,880**, followed by **APAC** with **448,785**.
- **Main App** is the top product with **405,645** in revenue.
- Monthly revenue peaked at **162,260** in **2023-03**.
- Monthly paid users peaked at **4,018** in **2023-03**.
- The strongest month-over-month revenue increase was **38.48%** in **2022-08**.
- The largest month-over-month decline was **-12.65%** in **2022-12**.
- New MRR reached its highest value of **44,085** in **2022-06**.
- Monthly ARPPU declined as the paid-user base expanded, indicating that user growth outpaced average revenue per payer.
